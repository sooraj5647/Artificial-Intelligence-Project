COMPUTER VISION


People generally use vision as their primary means of sensing their environment, we generally see more than we hear, feel or smell or taste. The goal of computer vision research is to give computers this same powerful facility for understanding their surrounding.

Examples of Computer Vision:

Face lock in smartphones:
Smartphones nowadays come with the feature of face locks in which the smartphone's owner can set up his/her face as unlocking mechanism for it. The front camera detects and captures the face and saves its features while initiation. Next time onwards, whenever the features match, the phone is unlocked.

Uses for Computer Vision technology include:

1. Autonomous vehicles
2. Google Translate app
3. Facial recognition
4. Gaming and controls
5. Surveillance
6. Emoji Scavenger Hunt:
A game based on Computer Vision where the machine initiates the game by showing an Emoji. The participant is expected to show a similar object in-front of the camera while the machine keeps on guessing what is being shown to it.
